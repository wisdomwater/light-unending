## 81 — Love on the Cross

> *"Behold, your mother!... Behold, your son!"* — John 19:26-27

Love reveals itself most clearly when life becomes difficult.

It is easy to imagine love as something reserved for peaceful moments, for celebrations, for days when there is abundance to share. Yet some of the deepest expressions of love emerge precisely when we feel emptied, wounded, or overwhelmed. In the midst of pain, the heart is given an opportunity to show what truly lives within it.

Even while suffering, the impulse of love continues to move outward. It notices the lonely. It gathers the scattered. It creates belonging where fear would create separation. Love does not become smaller when circumstances grow darker. It often becomes more visible.

There are moments when life feels like a crossroad of losses, disappointments, and uncertainties. In those moments, we may be tempted to withdraw into ourselves, protecting what little strength remains. Yet the deeper invitation is often toward connection. We discover that healing rarely comes through isolation. We are carried by relationships, kindness, friendship, and shared burdens.

The spiritual life is not merely about our relationship with God. It is also about discovering one another as gifts. Again and again, divine love forms people into family—not through bloodlines, agreement, or similarity, but through compassion. We belong to one another more deeply than we often realize.

Beneath all the divisions we create, there remains a quiet truth: every soul is held within the same love. The more we awaken to that reality, the more we become participants in the healing of the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me to love*
>
> Exhale: *Even in difficulty*
