## 37 — Beyond Literal Hunger

> *"The words that I speak to you are spirit, and are life."* — John 6:63

Much of life is spent learning to see beyond appearances. A gift is more than the object itself; it is an expression of love. A meal is more than nourishment; it is often a moment of connection. A conversation is more than words; it carries presence, understanding, and relationship.

The spiritual life invites the same kind of deeper seeing. We naturally begin with what is visible and tangible, yet there comes a point when we are asked to look beneath the surface. The symbols remain important, but they are no longer the destination. They become windows pointing toward a reality that cannot be contained by any single form.

Many of our struggles arise when we cling too tightly to the outer layer of things. We mistake the finger for the moon, the map for the landscape, the container for what it contains. The mind seeks certainty in what it can define, while the heart quietly longs for something larger, deeper, and more alive.

Spirit is not opposed to ordinary life. Rather, it is the hidden depth within ordinary life. It is the love moving beneath kindness, the beauty shining through creation, the sacred presence dwelling within every person. When we begin to perceive this deeper dimension, the world itself becomes more transparent to grace.

The invitation is not to abandon the visible world but to see through it. Every act of compassion, every shared meal, every moment of forgiveness, every breath becomes a doorway into a life that is richer than appearances alone can reveal. What nourishes the soul is not merely what we consume with our bodies or understand with our minds, but what awakens us to the living Presence at the heart of all things.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *To deeper life*
