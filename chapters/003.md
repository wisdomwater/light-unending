## 3 — The Word Beneath Every Word

> *“The Word was with God, and the Word was God.”* — John 1:1

Our lives are filled with words. We speak them, hear them, read them, and carry them within ourselves. Some words encourage us. Others wound us. Many become part of the stories we tell about who we are and how the world works. Over time, these countless voices can create a kind of inner crowd, each one demanding our attention.

Yet beneath all the words of the world is a deeper Word—not a sound carried through the air, but a living wisdom present within all things. It is the quiet invitation toward truth whenever we are tempted by illusion. It is the gentle movement toward compassion when resentment would be easier. It is the pull toward wholeness that remains even when our lives feel fragmented.

Most of the time, this deeper Voice does not compete with the noise around us. It rarely shouts. It does not force itself upon us. Instead, it waits patiently beneath our distractions, beneath our fears, beneath the endless stream of thoughts that pass through the mind. Like a spring flowing beneath the surface of the earth, it remains present whether we notice it or not.

There are moments when we become aware of it. Perhaps it comes during a quiet walk, a conversation filled with honesty, a moment of unexpected beauty, or a season when suffering strips away our usual certainties. For a brief instant, we sense something larger than ourselves. We feel called toward a deeper way of being. The heart recognizes what the mind struggles to explain.

This deeper Word is not foreign to us. It is woven into the very fabric of our lives. It speaks through love, through conscience, through wonder, through the longing for meaning and connection. Whenever we move toward what is life-giving, healing, and true, we are already responding to its call.

The invitation is simple. We do not need to manufacture this Presence or strain to hear it. We need only become quiet enough to listen. Beneath every word we speak and every story we tell, the eternal Word continues to whisper the same message: you belong, you are loved, and you are being drawn into a life larger than fear.
