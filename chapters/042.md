## 42 — Stones Dropped to the Ground

> *"They... being convicted by their conscience, went out one by one."* — John 8:9

There are moments when we become aware of how much energy we spend judging ourselves and others. We compare, measure, criticize, defend, and condemn. Often we do this without even noticing. The mind creates stories about who is worthy and who is not, who belongs and who does not, who deserves compassion and who deserves blame.

Yet beneath all these judgments lies a deeper truth: every human being carries both wounds and beauty, weakness and strength, fear and longing. The lines we draw between ourselves and others are rarely as clear as we imagine. The more honestly we see our own humanity, the more gently we begin to hold the humanity of everyone else.

The stones we carry are not always visible. Sometimes they are harsh opinions. Sometimes they are old resentments. Sometimes they are the impossible standards we place upon ourselves. We clutch them tightly because we believe they protect us, but they often become burdens that weigh down the heart.

Love invites a different way. Not the denial of harm, nor the avoidance of responsibility, but the recognition that healing grows best in the soil of mercy. When compassion enters the room, condemnation begins to lose its grip. The hands that were once clenched slowly begin to open.

Perhaps one of the great spiritual awakenings is realizing that we all stand in need of the same grace. The light does not shine on a chosen few while leaving others in darkness. It shines on everyone. When we see this clearly, the stones begin to fall from our hands, and we discover that freedom was waiting beneath them all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me mercy*
>
> Exhale: *Help me release judgment*
