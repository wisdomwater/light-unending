## 79 — The Freedom of Surrender

> *"Put the sword into its sheath."* — John 18:11

There are moments in life when every instinct urges us to fight. We want to defend ourselves, protect our image, control outcomes, or force events in a direction that feels safer. Fear tells us that survival depends on holding tighter, speaking louder, or striking first.

Yet there is a deeper strength that does not arise from resistance. It emerges when we stop clinging to what we cannot control and entrust ourselves to something larger than our fear. Surrender is often misunderstood as weakness, but true surrender requires immense courage. It asks us to release our grip on certainty and remain open to the unfolding of life.

Most of us carry invisible swords. Some are made of anger. Others are forged from anxiety, defensiveness, or the need to always be right. We reach for them whenever we feel threatened. Yet these weapons rarely bring peace. They may win arguments, but they often wound relationships. They may create temporary security, but they cannot heal the deeper unrest within us.

The soul discovers freedom when it no longer needs to fight every battle. This does not mean becoming passive or indifferent. It means learning to respond from love rather than fear. It means trusting that our worth does not depend upon our ability to control every circumstance.

Divine Presence is often found in the place where striving ends. Beneath all our efforts to manage life is a quiet invitation: to trust that Love remains present even when the path ahead is unclear. What we release into that Love is never truly lost. It is transformed.

Perhaps the deepest surrender is not giving up our strength but allowing our strength to be guided by wisdom. In that surrender, we discover a freedom no external circumstance can take away.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I release my grip*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Love*
