## 47 — The Healing of Vision

> *"Go, wash... So he went away, washed, and came back seeing."* — John 9:7

There are times when we long for change to happen instantly. We want clarity without confusion, healing without process, transformation without surrender. Yet much of life's deepest work unfolds gradually. We are invited to participate in our own awakening, taking one small step at a time toward the light we have been given.

The healing of vision is about more than correcting what the eyes can see. It is about learning to perceive reality more deeply. Many of us have been taught to view ourselves through the lens of shame, inadequacy, or fear. We carry old stories that tell us we are not enough, not worthy, not lovable. Over time, these stories become so familiar that we mistake them for truth.

Yet beneath those stories, something deeper remains untouched. The divine image within us is never lost. The light within the soul may be obscured, but it is never extinguished. Grace works patiently, often in ways we do not immediately recognize, washing away the layers that prevent us from seeing what has always been there.

As our vision heals, we begin to recognize the same sacred worth in others. We become less concerned with labels and appearances and more attentive to the hidden beauty carried by every person. Compassion grows where judgment once lived. Curiosity replaces certainty. Love expands beyond the boundaries we once believed were fixed.

The healing of vision is not a single moment but a lifelong unfolding. Day by day, we learn to see ourselves, others, and the world through the eyes of mercy. And as our sight clears, we discover that the Presence we were searching for has been walking beside us the entire time, quietly inviting us into a larger and more beautiful way of seeing.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Heal my vision*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me to see*
