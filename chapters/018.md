## 18 — The Wind That Cannot Be Controlled

> *"The wind blows where it wants to."* — John 3:8

We often wish the spiritual life were more predictable. We want clear formulas, reliable methods, and guaranteed outcomes. We would like to know exactly how transformation happens and how long it will take. Yet the deepest movements of the soul rarely follow our schedules.

The Spirit moves more like the wind than a machine. We cannot command it. We cannot contain it. We cannot force it to appear on demand. We may feel its presence one moment and wonder where it has gone the next. Yet even when unseen, it continues its quiet work.

Many of us spend great energy trying to control our lives. We try to manage every uncertainty, avoid every risk, and protect ourselves from disappointment. But love cannot flourish in a clenched hand. The heart grows most deeply when it learns to remain open to what it cannot fully predict.

The wind teaches us trust. We do not see it directly, yet we witness its effects. Leaves begin to dance. Branches begin to sway. The air itself seems to come alive. In much the same way, the presence of God is often recognized not through certainty but through its fruits. Greater compassion. Greater patience. Greater freedom. A growing capacity to love.

Sometimes the Spirit leads us beyond familiar territory. Old assumptions loosen. Long-held fears begin to dissolve. New possibilities emerge where we once saw only limitations. This can feel unsettling at first. Yet every living thing must move and grow. What remains rigid eventually becomes brittle. What remains open can be renewed.

The invitation is not to control the wind but to raise the sail. We cannot direct the breath of God, but we can become willing to receive it. When we do, we may discover that the same Presence we have been seeking has quietly been carrying us all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Spirit of freedom*
>
> Exhale: *Carry me where love leads*
