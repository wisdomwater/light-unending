## 85 — Recognized by Name

> *"Jesus said to her, 'Mary.'"* — John 20:16

There is a profound difference between being noticed and being known. Many of us spend our lives hoping someone will truly see us—not the roles we perform, the masks we wear, or the expectations we carry, but the person hidden beneath them all. Deep within every heart is a longing to be recognized in this way.

We often search for the Divine in dramatic experiences, extraordinary insights, or moments of certainty. Yet the sacred frequently appears in ordinary places. It stands quietly beside us in gardens of grief, in seasons of confusion, and in moments when tears blur our vision. Sometimes we do not recognize the Presence because it arrives in forms we were not expecting.

The turning point comes not through explanation but through relationship. A single word spoken with love can awaken something deeper than understanding. We recognize the voice because it has always been speaking beneath the noise. Long before we began searching for God, Love was searching for us. Long before we learned the language of prayer, the Divine already knew our name.

To be known in this way is not to be evaluated or judged. It is to be held within a gaze of compassion that sees everything and yet remains unwavering in its affection. Nothing needs to be hidden. Nothing needs to be earned. The deepest truth about us is not our failures, fears, or wounds. The deepest truth is that we belong.

Perhaps the spiritual journey is less about finding God and more about awakening to the One who has never lost sight of us. Again and again, amid the distractions and disappointments of life, Love calls our name. And each time we hear it, we discover that we are already closer to home than we imagined.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You know me*
>
> Exhale: *I belong to Love*
