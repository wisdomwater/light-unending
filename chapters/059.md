## 59 — Love Kneels Down

> *"You also ought to wash one another’s feet."* — John 13:14

Many of us imagine love as something grand. We think of dramatic sacrifices, heroic acts, or moments that change the course of a life. Yet love most often reveals itself in quieter ways. It appears in small kindnesses, patient listening, gentle words, and ordinary acts of care that may never be noticed by the world.

The deepest love does not need to elevate itself above others. It does not seek status, recognition, or control. Love is secure enough to move downward. It is willing to serve, to help, to carry burdens, and to enter the places where human need is greatest.

This movement can feel strange to the ego. Part of us longs to be important, admired, or seen as successful. Yet the soul discovers a different kind of freedom. There is a joy that arises when we stop asking how high we can climb and begin asking how deeply we can love.

Every day presents opportunities to kneel down. A difficult conversation. A family member who needs patience. A stranger who feels invisible. A friend carrying grief. These moments become sacred when we meet them with an open heart. What appears small in the eyes of the world often becomes immense in the kingdom of love.

The mystery is that when love kneels down, it does not become less. It becomes more fully itself. The heart that serves without seeking reward begins to participate in the very life of God, whose presence continually pours itself out for the healing of the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me to love*
>
> Exhale: *Through humble service*
