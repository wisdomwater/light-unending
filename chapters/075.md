## 75 — The Prayer of Oneness

> *"That they may all be one."* — John 17:21

Beneath nearly every human struggle lies a sense of separation. We feel separated from one another, separated from our own hearts, separated from the life we long for, and sometimes even separated from God. Much of our striving is an attempt to bridge these imagined distances.

Yet the deepest spiritual awakening is not discovering how to reach God. It is discovering that God has never been far away. The divine life flows through every breath, every moment, every living soul. What appears divided on the surface remains connected at a depth beyond words.

We see ourselves as isolated individuals moving through a crowded world, but love reveals a different reality. Beneath our fears, opinions, and identities, there is a shared belonging. We arise from the same mystery and are sustained by the same love. The barriers that seem so solid are often thinner than we imagine.

This does not erase our uniqueness. A forest is not made beautiful because every tree is identical. Its beauty comes from many distinct lives sharing the same soil, sunlight, and rain. Unity is not sameness. It is communion. It is learning to recognize ourselves in one another without losing ourselves.

The spiritual journey gradually softens the illusion of separation. We begin to notice that compassion feels more natural than judgment, forgiveness more liberating than resentment, and connection more life-giving than isolation. Love draws us toward wholeness because love recognizes what has always been true.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to rest in that deeper reality. Beneath every division, beneath every fear, beneath every label, there is a sacred belonging waiting to be remembered.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *We belong together*
>
> Exhale: *In the heart of Love*
