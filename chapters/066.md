## 66 — The Gift of Peace

> *"My peace I give to you."* — John 14:27

Peace is one of the things we long for most, yet often seek in the wrong places. We tell ourselves that peace will come when the problem is solved, when the uncertainty passes, when the relationship is healed, or when life finally unfolds according to plan. We postpone peace until some future moment when everything feels secure.

Yet the peace of the Spirit is different. It does not depend upon circumstances being favorable. It does not require every question to be answered or every storm to be calmed. It arises from a deeper place within us—a place rooted in the Presence that remains steady even when the surface of life is turbulent.

Most of us know what it feels like to carry a troubled heart. We worry about the future. We replay old conversations. We imagine losses that have not happened. Fear pulls us away from the present moment and scatters our attention in a hundred directions. The mind becomes crowded, and the soul grows weary.

The invitation is not to force ourselves into calmness but to return gently to what is already true. Beneath the noise, beneath the anxiety, beneath the endless stream of thoughts, there is a deeper current carrying us. Divine love has not abandoned us. We are held even when we feel unsteady. We are accompanied even when we feel alone.

This peace does not always remove pain, but it transforms how we carry it. It allows us to remain open when fear would have us close. It gives us the courage to trust what we cannot control. It reminds us that our lives rest within hands far larger than our own.

Little by little, we learn that peace is not something we manufacture. It is something we receive. It arrives when we loosen our grip, take a breath, and remember that the Love that sustains the stars is also sustaining us.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your peace is here*
>
