## 71 — Friends of God

> *"I have called you friends."* — John 15:15

Many people carry an image of God shaped by distance. God is imagined as a ruler far above, watching, evaluating, and issuing commands from afar. Whether we realize it or not, this image can leave us feeling perpetually inadequate, always trying to earn approval that never quite feels secure.

Yet there is another invitation. The sacred does not merely seek obedience; it seeks relationship. The voice of divine love calls us not servants, but friends.

Friendship is built on presence. A true friend does not stand over us but beside us. There is openness, trust, shared life, and mutual delight. We do not hide our fears from a trusted friend, nor do we pretend to be someone we are not. We come as we are, knowing we are welcomed.

This kind of friendship changes the way we approach the spiritual life. Prayer becomes less like presenting a report and more like sharing a conversation. Silence becomes companionship rather than emptiness. Even our struggles can become places of intimacy, because nothing needs to be concealed from the One who already knows us completely.

The remarkable mystery is that divine love has always moved toward us first. Before we began searching, we were being sought. Before we learned to trust, we were already trusted with existence itself. The invitation into friendship is not a reward for spiritual achievement. It is the very foundation upon which the journey begins.

As this friendship deepens, we gradually discover that we are living within a relationship larger than we imagined. The Love at the heart of reality is not indifferent to us. It knows us, welcomes us, and continually invites us closer. We are not merely tolerated in the presence of God. We are cherished there.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You call me friend*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Your love*
