## 54 — Come Out of the Tomb

> *"Lazarus, come out!"* — John 11:43

There are many kinds of tombs.

Some are built from grief that has never fully healed. Some are formed from fear, disappointment, shame, or exhaustion. Some are created so gradually that we hardly notice them. We withdraw a little. We stop hoping quite so much. We settle for survival when our hearts were made for life.

Often these inner tombs feel permanent. We become accustomed to the darkness. What once felt confining begins to feel familiar. We tell ourselves that this is simply who we are now. We lower our expectations. We stop listening for the possibility of change.

Yet the voice of Life continues to call.

The divine invitation does not come only to the strong, the successful, or the spiritually certain. It reaches into the hidden places we have abandoned. It calls to the parts of ourselves we thought were beyond recovery. It speaks gently but persistently, reminding us that no darkness has the final word.

The journey of awakening is often less about becoming someone new and more about stepping out of what has concealed our true life. The light does not create our deepest identity. It reveals it. Beneath the layers of fear and self-protection, something living remains. The image of God within us has never been extinguished.

The call to come forth may arrive as a small act of courage. A conversation long avoided. A step toward healing. A willingness to trust again. Rarely does transformation happen all at once. Yet every movement toward love is a movement out of the tomb and into the open air.

Life is continually calling us beyond our confinements. The door is not locked from the outside. The invitation is already being spoken. The question is not whether the voice is calling. The question is whether we are willing to take one step toward the light.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Call me into life*
>
> Exhale: *I step toward the light*
