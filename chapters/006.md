## 6 — Children of the Living Mystery

> *Our deepest identity is not something we achieve, but something we awaken to.*

Many of us spend years trying to answer the question of who we are. We build identities from our successes and failures, our family histories, our occupations, our beliefs, and the roles we play in the lives of others. Some of these things are meaningful and beautiful. Yet none of them can fully contain the mystery of what it means to be human.

Beneath every label and every story lies a deeper truth. We did not create ourselves. We did not manufacture the spark of life that burns within us. Before we accomplished anything, before we proved ourselves worthy, before we found the right words to describe our faith or our doubts, we were already held within a greater Love. Our truest identity begins there.

The spiritual journey often involves a gradual awakening to this reality. We begin to see that our worth is not earned through perfection, performance, or approval. It is not dependent upon how much we know, how successful we become, or how closely we match the expectations of others. There is something within us that is older and deeper than all of these things—a sacred belonging that cannot be taken away.

This does not mean that life becomes easy or that we stop growing. Rather, growth begins to emerge from a different foundation. Instead of striving to become worthy of love, we learn to live from the love that has already been given. Instead of constantly defending an identity built on fear, we begin to rest in an identity rooted in relationship. We discover that we are participants in a mystery far larger than ourselves.

When this awareness begins to take root, it changes the way we see others as well. Every person we encounter carries the same hidden dignity. Beneath their wounds, fears, and imperfections lives the same sacred longing for connection, meaning, and belonging. The more deeply we recognize this truth within ourselves, the more naturally compassion begins to flow toward others.

The invitation is not to become someone else. It is to awaken to what has always been true. We are not strangers wandering through a meaningless universe. We are children of the Living Mystery, continually called into deeper participation in a Love that knows us completely and welcomes us still.
