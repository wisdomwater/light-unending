## 8 — The God Who Comes Near

> *The heart of divine love is not distance, but presence.*

Many people imagine God as far away. We picture the sacred as existing somewhere beyond the world, beyond ordinary life, beyond the reach of human experience. When we feel lost, confused, or burdened by our failures, that distance can seem even greater. We may wonder whether we must somehow climb our way back to God through effort, knowledge, or spiritual achievement.

Yet the deepest spiritual insight is often the opposite. The divine does not wait at the end of a long journey. The Presence we seek continually moves toward us. Love takes the initiative. Grace arrives before we know how to ask for it. Again and again, the sacred enters the ordinary places of human life—not only our joys and triumphs, but also our struggles, uncertainties, and wounds.

This means that no part of life is too small or too ordinary to become a meeting place with God. The sacred is not confined to temples, holy days, or moments of profound spiritual experience. It can be encountered in a shared meal, a conversation with a friend, the laughter of a child, the beauty of a sunset, or the quiet stillness of an ordinary morning. The divine continually chooses nearness.

There is something deeply comforting in this realization. We do not need to hide our humanity in order to be loved. We do not need to become flawless before we are welcomed into divine presence. The God who comes near is not repelled by weakness, doubt, grief, or imperfection. Instead, divine love enters those very places, illuminating them from within and gently drawing us toward healing.

As we begin to trust this nearness, our understanding of the spiritual life changes. It becomes less about escaping the world and more about awakening within it. We stop searching for God only in extraordinary moments and begin noticing the countless ways Love is already present. The sacred is not merely waiting beyond the horizon. It is woven into the fabric of everyday life.

The invitation is simple and profound: become present to the Presence that is already here. The God who comes near is nearer than our fears, nearer than our thoughts, nearer even than the breath moving in and out of our bodies. We are surrounded by a Love that continually enters our lives, not to condemn or control, but to dwell with us and lead us gently into deeper life.
