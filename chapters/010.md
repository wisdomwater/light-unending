## 10 — The Lamb in Our Midst

> *Love often arrives with such gentleness that we overlook its presence.*

Many people imagine that the Divine would reveal itself through overwhelming displays of power. We look for certainty, spectacle, and unmistakable signs. We assume that if God were truly near, the evidence would be impossible to miss. Yet again and again, the sacred appears in quieter ways. It comes clothed in humility, tenderness, compassion, and self-giving love.

The heart is often searching for something extraordinary while overlooking the holy presence already woven into ordinary life. We hurry through our days waiting for some future revelation, not realizing that grace has been accompanying us all along. The Divine frequently stands among us hidden in simple moments: a forgiving conversation, a shared meal, an unexpected kindness, a burden carried together, a moment of deep stillness that arrives without explanation.

The image of the lamb speaks to a mystery that runs through the entire spiritual journey. The deepest power in the universe is not domination but love. It does not conquer by force. It heals by drawing near. It transforms not through coercion but through willing self-giving. What appears weak often carries a strength far greater than anything fear can produce.

We witness this truth whenever love enters places of division and slowly softens what seemed immovable. A harsh heart becomes gentler. An old wound begins to heal. A long-held resentment loosens its grip. These changes rarely happen through pressure alone. More often, they unfold because someone was willing to remain present, patient, and compassionate. Love absorbs what fear cannot overcome.

The invitation is not merely to admire this way of being but to recognize it within our own lives. The same Spirit that moves toward healing, reconciliation, and wholeness is continually at work within every human heart. Whenever we choose compassion over judgment, understanding over suspicion, or forgiveness over retaliation, we participate in that sacred movement.

Perhaps the greatest wonder is that this loving Presence is not far away. It is already here, quietly moving through the world, gently calling creation toward restoration. The question is not whether love is present. The question is whether we will slow down enough to recognize the Lamb already in our midst.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Love is here*
>
> Exhale: *Open my eyes*
