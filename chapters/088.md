## 88 — Breakfast by the Sea

> *"Jesus said to them, 'Come and eat breakfast!'"* — John 21:12

There are seasons when life feels full of certainty and purpose. There are also seasons when we quietly return to familiar routines because we do not know what else to do. We go back to work. Back to responsibilities. Back to ordinary days. We keep moving, even while carrying unanswered questions within us.

Yet it is often in those ordinary places that grace appears.

The Divine rarely waits for us only in mountaintop experiences or moments of spiritual clarity. More often, Presence meets us beside the waters of everyday life—in kitchens, offices, living rooms, hospital rooms, and quiet morning walks. We may not recognize it at first. We may be tired, distracted, or discouraged. Still, Love stands on the shoreline waiting for us.

What is striking is that breakfast is already prepared before the disciples arrive. The fire is burning. The bread is ready. The meal is waiting. Before they can offer anything, they are welcomed. Before they can explain themselves, they are invited to sit down. This is the way of grace. We are often busy trying to earn what has already been given.

Many of us imagine that spiritual life begins when we finally become worthy enough, wise enough, or faithful enough. Yet the deeper mystery is that God is already present before our searching begins. The invitation comes first. The welcome comes first. Love precedes every response we make.

The voice that calls, "Come and eat," still echoes through every human life. It is an invitation to stop striving for a moment and simply receive. To sit near the fire. To rest in a Presence that knows us completely. To discover that we were never forgotten, even when we felt lost at sea.

**Breath Prayer**
