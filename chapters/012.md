## 12 — Come and See

> *“Come and see.”* — John 1:39

There is something deeply human about wanting certainty before taking a step. We want to know where the road leads, what challenges lie ahead, and whether the journey will be worth the effort. We seek assurances that our hopes will not be disappointed and that our trust will not be misplaced. Yet much of what matters most in life cannot be discovered from a distance.

Love is like this. Friendship is like this. Beauty is like this. No amount of explanation can replace direct experience. We may read about the ocean, study maps of distant lands, or listen to others describe what they have seen, but eventually there comes a moment when we must step forward and experience it for ourselves.

The spiritual life unfolds in much the same way. We often search for answers, doctrines, techniques, or certainties that will remove all mystery from the journey. Yet the deepest invitation is rarely an explanation. It is an encounter. We are not asked to solve the mystery of God before drawing near. We are simply invited to come closer.

This invitation meets us exactly where we are. We do not need perfect faith. We do not need complete understanding. We do not need to have resolved every doubt or answered every question. The doorway stands open to the curious, the weary, the hopeful, the skeptical, and the searching alike. Love does not wait for us to become ready. It welcomes us as we are.

As we respond, we often discover that the journey itself becomes the teacher. What once seemed uncertain gradually becomes familiar. What once felt distant becomes intimate. We begin to notice traces of the sacred woven throughout ordinary life—in moments of silence, acts of compassion, unexpected beauty, and quiet inner awakenings. The Presence we sought in far-off places reveals itself in the very texture of our daily lives.

Perhaps this is the hidden gift of the invitation. We begin by seeking something beyond ourselves, only to discover that the One we seek has already been accompanying us. The path is not about earning nearness to God but awakening to a nearness that has always been there. And so the invitation remains as simple and as profound as ever: come and see.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I draw near*
>
> Exhale: *Come and see*
