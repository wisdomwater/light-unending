## 77 — That They May Be One

> *"I in them, and you in me, that they may be perfected into one."* — John 17:23

There is a longing woven into the human heart that no accomplishment can fully satisfy. We seek connection in friendships, family, romance, community, and shared purpose because something within us knows we were not made for isolation. We ache for a belonging deeper than proximity—a union of hearts.

Yet true unity cannot be manufactured. It does not arise from everyone thinking alike, agreeing on everything, or fitting into the same mold. Whenever we try to force unity from the outside, it eventually fractures. Real oneness grows from within, like roots intertwining beneath the surface long before a forest appears connected above ground.

The deeper we travel into the heart, the more we discover that love is not merely something we practice. It is something we participate in. We are drawn into a life larger than our individual selves. The boundaries that once seemed so absolute become more permeable. Compassion begins to flow more freely. The joys and sorrows of others no longer feel entirely separate from our own.

This does not mean losing our individuality. A river remains made of countless drops of water, yet together they become something capable of nourishing entire landscapes. The mystery of divine life is that we become most fully ourselves not by standing apart, but by learning how deeply we belong to one another.

Perhaps this is part of what it means to mature spiritually. The self becomes less defended and more open. The heart becomes less occupied with protecting its territory and more willing to participate in the great exchange of giving and receiving love. What once felt like a collection of separate lives begins to reveal itself as a shared journey.

Every act of kindness, every moment of forgiveness, every choice to see another person's dignity strengthens this hidden unity. Little by little, the world becomes more whole as we learn to live from the love that already holds us together.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Make us one in love*
>
> Exhale: *Teach us to belong*
