## 67 — Never Left Alone

> *"I will not leave you orphans."* — John 14:18

One of the deepest fears carried by the human heart is the fear of abandonment. We fear being forgotten, overlooked, or left to face life on our own. Even when surrounded by people, there can be moments when we feel profoundly alone, as though no one truly sees the burdens we carry or understands the path we walk.

Yet beneath that fear lies a deeper reality. The Presence that gives life to all things has never withdrawn from us. Even when we feel lost, the divine life remains closer than our breath, quietly sustaining us from within. Our awareness may come and go, but love itself does not depart.

There are seasons when familiar sources of comfort disappear. A relationship changes. A loved one dies. A cherished belief no longer carries us as it once did. During such times, it can feel as though the ground beneath us is giving way. Yet these very moments sometimes become doorways into a deeper discovery. What we thought was holding us was never the ultimate source of our life.

The Spirit meets us not only in moments of certainty but also in moments of unknowing. A quiet strength appears when our own strength is exhausted. A gentle wisdom arises when we no longer know what to do. A sense of companionship emerges that cannot be explained, only experienced. Something within us recognizes that we are being carried.

The journey of awakening is, in part, the gradual realization that we belong to a Love that never lets go. We may wander. We may doubt. We may lose sight of the light for a time. Yet the light never loses sight of us. The One we seek is already seeking us, patiently waiting beneath every distraction and every fear.

You are not abandoned. You are not forgotten. You are not making this journey alone. Beneath every changing circumstance, beneath every season of life, there remains an unbroken Presence whispering the same eternal truth: you are loved, and you belong.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I am not alone*
>
> Exhale: *Your presence remains*
