## 2 — Before the Beginning

> *"The Word was with God, and the Word was God."* — John 1:1

Human beings live within time. We measure our lives through beginnings and endings, milestones and memories, arrivals and departures. We remember where we came from, wonder where we are going, and often feel caught between what has been and what might yet be. Yet there is a deeper reality beneath the flow of time itself—something that does not come and go.

Before there were nations and civilizations, before there were mountains and oceans, before there was even a world to inhabit, there was the eternal mystery of divine life. At the heart of existence is not loneliness, conflict, or emptiness, but relationship. Love did not emerge as a response to creation. Love was already present before anything was made.

This can be difficult for the mind to grasp because we are accustomed to thinking in sequences. We ask what came first, what caused what followed, and where everything began. Yet the deepest truths often lie beyond the reach of such questions. The Source of all things is not merely older than the universe. It is the living Presence from which every moment continually arises.

When we touch this mystery, even briefly, something within us begins to relax. We no longer need to carry the burden of holding reality together. We are not accidental beings struggling for significance in an indifferent universe. We are participants in a life that began long before us and will continue long beyond us—a life whose deepest nature is generosity, creativity, and communion.

This realization can change the way we see ourselves. If Love existed before the beginning, then love is not a fragile human invention. It is woven into the fabric of reality itself. Every act of kindness, every movement of compassion, every longing for connection resonates with something ancient and eternal. We are not creating the song; we are learning to hear the music that has always been playing.

The spiritual journey invites us to rest in that larger story. Beneath our fears, beneath our striving, beneath the countless narratives we tell about ourselves, there remains a Presence that was here before the beginning and remains with us now. To awaken to that Presence is to discover that we have always been held within a Love without origin and without end.
