## 72 — Sorrow Becoming Joy

> *"Your sorrow will be turned into joy."* — John 16:20

There are seasons when life seems to move through a valley we did not choose. Something precious is lost. A relationship changes. A dream dissolves. A certainty that once anchored us no longer holds. In such moments, it can feel as though the light has withdrawn and left us alone in the dark.

Yet many of the deepest transformations begin this way. The seed disappears into the earth before it becomes a tree. Dawn arrives only after the longest hours of night. What appears to be an ending is often a hidden beginning.

We naturally resist sorrow because we imagine it has come to take something from us. Sometimes it does. But sorrow can also be a doorway. It softens what has become rigid. It opens places within us that comfort and success often leave untouched. Through grief, compassion grows. Through loss, we discover what truly matters. Through longing, the heart learns how deeply it was made for love.

The great mystery is that joy is not always the opposite of sorrow. Often the two become intertwined. The people who carry the deepest joy are rarely those who have avoided suffering. More often, they are those who have passed through it and discovered a Presence that remained with them in the midst of it.

Nothing painful lasts forever. Even when we cannot yet see how, life continues moving toward healing. Love continues its patient work beneath the surface. What feels unfinished today may one day reveal itself as part of a larger wholeness that was quietly unfolding all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Even here in sorrow*
>
> Exhale: *Joy is being born*
