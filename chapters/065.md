## 65 — Seeing the Father Everywhere

> *"He who has seen me has seen the Father."* — John 14:9

Many people imagine God as distant, hidden somewhere beyond the reach of ordinary life. We search for signs, visions, or extraordinary experiences that might finally reveal the divine to us. Yet again and again, the deepest spiritual wisdom points us back to what is already before our eyes.

If we wish to know the heart of God, we need not look toward power, domination, or fear. We look toward love. We look toward compassion that refuses to abandon the suffering. We look toward mercy that welcomes the excluded. We look toward forgiveness that breaks cycles of resentment and violence. Wherever love heals, restores, and gives life, something of the divine nature is being revealed.

The challenge is that we often overlook the sacred because it arrives in ordinary forms. We expect thunder and receive kindness. We expect certainty and receive presence. We expect a distant ruler and encounter a love that kneels, serves, and draws near. The holy frequently appears disguised as the everyday.

As our hearts awaken, we begin to notice traces of divine beauty everywhere. In the patience of a friend. In the courage of someone carrying a heavy burden. In the laughter of children. In the quiet care of those who serve without recognition. Small acts of love become windows through which eternal light shines.

This does not mean that everything reflects God equally clearly. The world still contains cruelty, fear, and brokenness. Yet even there, love continues its patient work of healing. The light has never stopped shining. It continues to appear in countless places, inviting us to see more deeply than appearances alone.

The more we learn to recognize divine love, the more the world becomes transparent to grace. What once seemed ordinary begins to glow with meaning. We discover that heaven is not merely somewhere else. It is breaking through everywhere love is allowed to flourish.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *To see Your love everywhere*
