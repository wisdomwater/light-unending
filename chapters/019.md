## 19 — The God Who Loves the World

> *"For God so loved the world..."* — John 3:16

The deepest truth about God is not power, judgment, or distance. It is love. Before any striving, before any seeking, before any spiritual achievement, there is already a love flowing toward creation. The universe itself rests within that embrace.

Many people carry hidden images of a reluctant God—one who must be persuaded, appeased, or convinced to draw near. Yet divine love moves in the opposite direction. It is always taking the first step. It is always reaching outward. Like sunlight pouring across a landscape, it shines without demanding that the flowers earn its warmth.

Notice that the object of this love is not a select few, but the world. The whole human family. Every nation, every culture, every personality, every wandering soul. The love of God is larger than our categories and wider than our boundaries. Wherever life exists, love is already present, calling it toward wholeness.

This does not mean that everything within us is already healed. We know the reality of fear, selfishness, and separation. Yet love does not respond to brokenness by abandoning it. Love moves toward what is wounded. It seeks restoration rather than rejection. The heart of God is not turned away from the world’s pain but turned toward it.

When we awaken to this love, something within us begins to soften. The need to prove ourselves gradually gives way to trust. The fear of exclusion begins to loosen. We discover that we are not standing outside the circle of grace trying to gain entrance. We have always been held within a mercy deeper than we knew.

The spiritual journey begins anew each time we remember this simple truth: the source of all things is love, and that love has never stopped pouring itself into the world. The invitation is not to earn it, but to receive it—and having received it, to let it flow through us to others.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You love the world*
>
