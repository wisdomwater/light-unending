## 70 — Fruit That Grows Naturally

> *"That you bear much fruit..."* — John 15:8

We often approach transformation as a project. We make lists of virtues to cultivate and flaws to overcome. We measure our progress and wonder whether we are becoming the person we hope to be. Yet the deepest changes in life rarely happen through force.

Consider a tree in springtime. It does not strain to produce blossoms. It does not anxiously calculate how many leaves it should grow. Rooted in good soil and nourished by sun and rain, it simply expresses the life flowing through it. Fruit appears as the natural outcome of connection.

The same is true of the soul. Love, patience, generosity, compassion, and wisdom are not trophies earned through spiritual effort. They are the natural fruit of a life rooted in divine love. As we open ourselves to that love, something within us begins to soften and expand. What once felt forced gradually becomes spontaneous.

This does not mean growth is always easy. Seasons of pruning, loss, and uncertainty often become the very places where deeper life emerges. Yet even these experiences can serve a hidden purpose. What seems like diminishment may be making room for greater freedom, greater tenderness, and greater trust.

When we stop obsessing over the fruit, we become more attentive to the roots. We learn to dwell in the Presence that sustains us. We learn to receive rather than grasp. And over time, often without noticing it, our lives begin to nourish others.

The world does not need more striving souls trying to appear spiritual. It needs people who are deeply rooted in love. Wherever love is allowed to flow freely, good fruit will follow in its own season.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Root me in love*
>
> Exhale: *Let good fruit grow*
