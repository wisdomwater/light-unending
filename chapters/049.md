## 49 — The Door Always Open

> *“I am the door. If anyone enters in by me...”* — John 10:9

There are seasons when the spiritual life feels like standing outside a locked gate. We wonder whether we are worthy enough, certain enough, faithful enough, or good enough to draw near to God. We imagine barriers where none were intended. We search for passwords, secret knowledge, or some future version of ourselves that will finally be accepted.

Yet the invitation of divine love is astonishingly simple. The door is already open.

Much of our suffering comes from believing we must force our way into a reality that is already embracing us. We strive to reach what has never stopped reaching toward us. We exhaust ourselves trying to earn what can only be received. Like a person standing in sunlight while searching for the sun, we overlook the very Presence surrounding us.

The doorway into spiritual life is not achievement but trust. It is the quiet willingness to step beyond fear and into relationship. Again and again, love invites us to leave the cramped rooms of anxiety, shame, and self-judgment and enter a wider landscape of belonging.

This doorway is not reserved for a select few. It stands open for every weary traveler, every seeker, every doubter, every soul longing for home. The invitation does not depend upon having all the answers. It begins with a simple turning of the heart toward the One who has been quietly waiting all along.

Whenever we choose love over fear, compassion over judgment, forgiveness over resentment, and trust over striving, we find ourselves passing through that doorway once more. What waits on the other side is not exclusion, but life. Not condemnation, but welcome. Not distance, but the discovery that God was nearer than we imagined from the very beginning.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *The door is open*
>
> Exhale: *I enter in trust*
