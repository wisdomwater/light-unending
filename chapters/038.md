## 38 — Staying When Others Leave

> *"Lord, to whom would we go? You have the words of eternal life."* — John 6:68

There are moments on every spiritual journey when the path becomes difficult. What once felt clear becomes mysterious. Old certainties begin to loosen. Questions arise that cannot be quickly answered. The heart finds itself standing in unfamiliar territory.

Many people assume that faith is the absence of doubt, but often the deeper journey looks very different. It is possible to be full of questions and still remain open. It is possible to feel uncertain and yet continue walking. Love does not require that we understand everything before taking the next step.

There are seasons when we are tempted to turn back toward simpler answers or easier paths. The unknown can feel uncomfortable. Mystery can feel unsettling. Yet something within us recognizes that we have already tasted a deeper reality. We may not be able to explain it fully, but we know we have encountered something true.

The spiritual life is not sustained by certainty alone. It is sustained by relationship. We continue because we have experienced moments of grace that changed us. We continue because we have glimpsed a love larger than fear. We continue because something within our deepest being resonates with the call toward wholeness, compassion, and life.

In the end, staying is not about having every question resolved. It is about trusting the quiet wisdom that has guided us this far. When the road becomes difficult, when others walk away, when mystery remains mystery, the heart can still say yes. Not because it possesses all the answers, but because it has come to recognize the voice of life when it hears it.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *In mystery I remain*
>
> Exhale: *In love I trust*
