## 46 — Learning to See

> *"Though I was blind, now I see."* — John 9:25

Much of life is spent learning to see. Not merely with our eyes, but with our hearts. We move through the world carrying assumptions, fears, wounds, expectations, and stories about ourselves and others. Often we do not realize how much these unseen lenses shape what we experience. We think we are seeing reality clearly, yet much remains hidden beneath the surface.

The awakening of the soul is often less dramatic than we imagine. It rarely arrives as perfect certainty or complete understanding. More often, it begins with a small opening. A glimpse of beauty where we expected emptiness. A moment of compassion where we expected judgment. A quiet awareness that we are more deeply loved than we previously believed.

As vision deepens, the world itself begins to change. Not because the world has changed, but because we are seeing it differently. We notice the sacred hidden within ordinary moments. We become more aware of the struggles others carry. We discover that every person contains a depth that cannot be measured by appearances alone. The Light that seemed absent is revealed to have been present all along.

True seeing is not about possessing all the answers. In fact, it often begins when we release our need to know everything. Humility clears the eyes. Wonder softens the heart. The soul becomes willing to encounter life as mystery rather than problem, gift rather than obstacle.

The journey of faith may be less about acquiring new beliefs and more about allowing our vision to be healed. Little by little, we awaken to the Presence that surrounds us, sustains us, and quietly calls us into greater love. What once seemed ordinary becomes luminous. What once felt separate becomes connected. What once appeared dark begins to shine.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *To the Light within*
