## 23 — Nothing Left to Hide

> *"He told me everything that I have done."* — John 4:39

There is a deep weariness that comes from hiding.

Many of us carry parts of our story that we would rather keep unseen. Regrets, failures, wounds, fears, and old memories can become burdens we quietly protect from the gaze of others. We learn to present the polished version of ourselves while guarding the places that still feel unfinished or broken.

Yet beneath the effort to hide is often a deeper fear: that if we were fully known, we would no longer be loved.

The Divine invitation moves in the opposite direction. Love does not require disguise. The Presence that sustains us already knows every chapter of our story, every joy and every sorrow, every act of courage and every moment of weakness. Nothing needs to be explained away. Nothing needs to be concealed. We are known before we ever begin to tell our story.

This kind of knowing is not harsh or condemning. It is healing. What love illuminates, it does not do so in order to shame. Light reveals so that restoration can begin. The places we fear most are often the very places where grace longs to enter. What we bring into the light loses the power it once held over us.

As we discover ourselves held within this compassionate gaze, something begins to soften. We spend less energy protecting an image and more energy living authentically. We become free to tell the truth about our lives. Free to acknowledge our wounds. Free to celebrate our gifts. Free to be human.

The spiritual journey is not about becoming someone else. It is about becoming transparent to the love that has always surrounded us. The more deeply we trust that love, the less we need to hide. We discover that what we feared would separate us becomes the very place where mercy meets us.

There comes a quiet freedom when there is nothing left to hide. Not because we are perfect, but because we are loved. And in that love, the soul finally learns to rest.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I am fully known*
>
> Exhale: *I am fully loved*
