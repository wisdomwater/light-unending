## 32 — Life Flowing Through the Son

> *"As the Father has life in himself, even so he gave to the Son also to have life in himself."* — John 5:26

Life is not something we possess so much as something we participate in.

We often imagine ourselves as separate beings, striving to secure meaning, purpose, and fulfillment through our own efforts. Yet beneath all our striving is a deeper reality. The breath within us is not self-created. The love we experience is not self-generated. Every moment of beauty, compassion, creativity, and awakening flows from a source deeper than the individual self.

The image of divine life flowing through the Son reveals a mystery at the heart of existence. Love pours itself out without ceasing. Life shares itself freely. Nothing is hoarded. Nothing is withheld. The movement of the divine is one of continual giving, continual relationship, continual communion. The life of God is not static but endlessly overflowing.

This same current flows through all creation. We glimpse it whenever kindness emerges where bitterness might have prevailed. We feel it when forgiveness softens an old wound. We encounter it in moments of wonder, silence, gratitude, and joy. The sacred life that animated saints and prophets is not absent from our own lives. It quietly sustains us even when we are unaware of its presence.

The spiritual journey is often less about acquiring something new than awakening to what has always been here. We begin to recognize that we live within a larger life. We are branches connected to a living vine, waves arising from a vast ocean, sparks held within an eternal fire. The more we open ourselves to this reality, the more freely love can move through us.

And this is the invitation: not merely to admire divine life from a distance, but to participate in it. To become receptive to the love that is already seeking expression through us. To discover that beneath our fears, our striving, and our sense of separation, there is a deeper stream carrying us toward wholeness. Life is flowing still, and we are invited to enter its current.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Life flows through me*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in the Source*
