## 24 — Worship Beyond Mountains

> *"The true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth."* — John 4:23

There is a stage of the spiritual journey when we believe we must go somewhere to find God. We look for the right place, the right tradition, the right practice, the right teacher, or the right set of beliefs. We imagine that the sacred lives on distant mountaintops and waits for us to arrive.

Yet over time, something deeper begins to awaken.

We discover that no building can contain the Infinite and no location can monopolize the Presence that gives life to all things. The Holy One we seek is not confined to a sanctuary, a nation, a denomination, or a sacred landscape. The Divine is nearer than breath, closer than thought, present in every moment and available in every place.

To worship in spirit is to become aware of this living Presence. It is to turn the attention of the heart toward the One who is already here. The soul no longer seeks God as an object far away but awakens to a communion that has always existed beneath the surface of ordinary life.

To worship in truth is to come honestly. No performance is required. No spiritual mask is needed. We do not have to appear stronger, wiser, or more faithful than we are. Truth begins when we stop pretending and allow ourselves to stand before God exactly as we are. In that honesty, a deeper intimacy becomes possible.

This kind of worship can happen anywhere. It can arise in a quiet room before dawn, during a walk beneath the trees, while washing dishes, sitting beside a hospital bed, or watching the evening sky fade into darkness. Wherever the heart opens to love, worship begins.

The great surprise of the spiritual life is that the distance between ourselves and God is often far smaller than we imagined. The mountain we thought we needed to climb was never the destination. The Presence we were searching for was already waiting within and around us, inviting us into a deeper awareness of what has always been true.
