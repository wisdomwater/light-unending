## 86 — Peace Be With You

> *"Peace be to you."* — John 20:19

There are seasons when we live behind locked doors. The doors may not be made of wood and iron, but of fear, disappointment, exhaustion, or uncertainty. We retreat into ourselves, hoping to protect what remains tender and vulnerable. The heart learns to build walls whenever life has wounded it deeply enough.

Yet Divine Presence has never been limited by the barriers we create. Love does not stand outside waiting for us to become fearless before drawing near. It enters the very places we have tried to seal off. It comes into the rooms where anxiety has taken up residence and where hope feels fragile. It meets us exactly where we are.

The first gift offered is peace. Not the fragile peace that depends upon circumstances going our way, but a deeper peace that arises from knowing we are not alone. This peace does not deny pain. It does not pretend suffering is unreal. Instead, it gently reminds us that something greater than fear is present within us and around us.

As this peace settles into the heart, it begins to transform how we see others. We discover that forgiveness becomes possible. Compassion grows where resentment once lived. The love we receive naturally seeks expression through the love we extend. The breath of God moves through human lives, carrying healing into a world that longs for it.

The spiritual life is not an escape from the world but a participation in its restoration. We are invited to become bearers of the same peace we have received. Every act of kindness, every word of encouragement, every gesture of mercy becomes a doorway through which divine love enters another person's life.

The doors may still appear closed. The challenges may not disappear overnight. Yet peace remains available even there. Love continues to arrive, uninvited yet deeply welcome, speaking to the fearful places within us: Peace be with you.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Peace enters here*
>
> Exhale: *Fear can rest*
