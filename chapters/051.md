## 51 — Abundant Life

> *“I came that they may have life, and may have it abundantly.”* — John 10:10

Many people spend years chasing life while somehow missing it. We search for fulfillment in achievement, security, recognition, possessions, or certainty, hoping that one more accomplishment will finally quiet the restless longing within. Yet even when we obtain what we sought, the deeper hunger often remains.

The life of the spirit points toward something different. Abundant life is not simply having more. It is awakening to the richness already present within and around us. It is learning to inhabit this moment more fully, to recognize the sacred hidden within ordinary days, and to discover that love is flowing beneath the surface of things.

This abundance does not depend upon perfect circumstances. It can be found in seasons of joy and in seasons of grief. It appears in laughter shared with friends, in quiet walks beneath the sky, in acts of kindness freely given, and in moments when the heart suddenly becomes aware of a Presence larger than itself. Life becomes abundant whenever we become deeply available to it.

Fear often convinces us that life is scarce. It tells us there is not enough love, enough time, enough goodness, enough hope. Love speaks another language. Love expands what fear contracts. It opens the clenched hand. It softens the guarded heart. It teaches us that the deepest treasures cannot be hoarded because they grow through sharing.

The invitation of abundant life is not merely about the future. It is here now. Each moment carries the possibility of awakening. Each breath offers another opportunity to participate in the life that continually flows from the heart of God into creation. We do not have to manufacture this life. We only need to become willing to receive it.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Life flows through me*
>
> Exhale: *I receive Your abundance*
