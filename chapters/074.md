## 74 — Peace in the Midst of Trouble

> *"In me you may have peace."* — John 16:33

Many of us spend our lives waiting for peace to arrive. We tell ourselves that once the problem is solved, the conflict resolved, the diagnosis healed, the bills paid, or the uncertainty removed, then we will finally be able to rest. Yet life has a way of presenting one wave after another. If peace depends upon calm circumstances, it will always remain just beyond our reach.

There is another kind of peace that does not wait for the storm to pass. It emerges from a deeper place within us—a place untouched by the constant rise and fall of events. Beneath our fears, beneath our worries about the future, beneath the countless stories we tell ourselves, there remains a quiet center where divine Presence dwells.

This peace is not denial. It does not pretend that suffering is unreal or that hardship does not matter. It allows us to fully acknowledge pain while remembering that pain is not the deepest truth about existence. Beneath every wound remains a love that has never abandoned us. Beneath every loss remains a life that continues to flow.

When we remember this deeper reality, we begin to meet life's challenges differently. We no longer need to control everything. We no longer need every outcome to unfold according to our plans. We can take the next faithful step and trust that we are being held even when the path ahead is unclear.

The peace of God is not the absence of trouble. It is the discovery that we are never facing trouble alone. A Presence greater than fear walks with us through every valley, every uncertainty, and every ending. And because love remains, hope remains. Because love remains, peace remains.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your peace is here*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in love*
