## 11 — What Are You Seeking?

> *“What are you looking for?”* — John 1:38

At some point in life, every person becomes aware of a quiet longing beneath the surface of ordinary existence. Even when our days are full, something within us continues searching. We seek happiness, meaning, belonging, peace, purpose, understanding, or love. Sometimes we can name what we are looking for. Often we cannot. We only feel the ache itself.

Much of our lives are spent chasing answers to desires we do not fully understand. We imagine that the next achievement, relationship, possession, insight, or experience will finally satisfy the restlessness within us. Yet each accomplishment eventually reveals its limitations. The deeper longing remains, gently inviting us beyond the surface of things.

The spiritual journey often begins when we become willing to sit honestly with the question. Rather than rushing toward quick answers, we pause long enough to listen to the deeper movements of the heart. What are we truly seeking? Beneath our desire for success, are we longing to be seen? Beneath our need for certainty, are we longing for trust? Beneath our search for belonging, are we longing to discover that we have always belonged?

The beautiful mystery is that our seeking is never a one-sided pursuit. The longing we feel may itself be evidence of a greater Love drawing us forward. We search because something within us recognizes a home it has not yet fully remembered. The hunger for meaning, beauty, truth, and connection is not a flaw to overcome. It is part of the sacred invitation woven into human life.

Yet the answer to our deepest longing is rarely delivered as a formula or explanation. Instead, we are invited into relationship and experience. The way unfolds through participation. We learn trust by trusting. We learn love by loving. We learn presence by becoming present. The path is discovered by walking it.

When we bring our seeking into stillness, we may begin to notice something surprising. Beneath all our questions rests a Presence that has never left us. The One we have been searching for has been quietly accompanying us from the beginning. What began as a search gradually becomes a recognition. We discover that the deepest longing of the heart and the deepest reality of existence have always belonged together.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *What am I seeking?*
>
> Exhale: *Lead me into truth*
