## 33 — Enough for Everyone

> *"They all ate as much as they desired."* — John 6:11

One of the deepest fears carried by the human heart is the fear that there will not be enough. Not enough time. Not enough resources. Not enough love. Not enough worth. We learn to live cautiously, protecting what we have and worrying about what might run out tomorrow.

Yet the deeper spiritual life continually invites us into another way of seeing. Beneath the anxiety of scarcity lies a reality of abundance. Life itself is a gift that arrives moment by moment. Breath is given. Light is given. Love is given. We did not create these things, yet they continually arrive.

Often we look at our lives the way the disciples looked at the small meal before them. We see only our limitations. We count our strengths and weaknesses. We measure what we possess against the vast needs around us. What we have seems too small to matter.

But love has a mysterious way of multiplying what is freely offered. A small kindness becomes a turning point in someone's life. A word of encouragement lingers in a wounded heart for years. A simple act of generosity creates ripples we never witness. What seems insignificant in our hands becomes something larger when surrendered to a deeper purpose.

The kingdom of love does not operate according to the mathematics of fear. It does not begin with counting what is lacking. It begins with gratitude for what is already present. When we stop staring at scarcity and begin participating in love, we often discover that more is available than we imagined.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to trust that what has been given to you is enough for this moment. Enough light for the next step. Enough strength for today's journey. Enough grace for your mistakes. Enough love to share. The Source from which all life flows has not forgotten you, and its abundance has not been exhausted.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *There is enough*
>
> Exhale: *Love will provide*
