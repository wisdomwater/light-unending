## 61 — The New Commandment

> *"Love one another, just as I have loved you."* — John 13:34

We often imagine spiritual growth as gaining more knowledge, mastering more practices, or achieving greater certainty. Yet beneath all the complexity of religion and philosophy, a remarkably simple invitation remains: learn to love.

Not a love that is merely sentimental or selective, but a love that continues even when it is inconvenient. A love that sees the dignity hidden within every person. A love that refuses to reduce others to their failures, labels, or differences. The kind of love that recognizes we are all traveling through the same mystery together.

This love does not arise from force of will alone. The more deeply we encounter divine love, the more naturally it begins to flow outward. We become channels rather than manufacturers of compassion. What we have freely received, we gradually learn to freely give.

The world often teaches us to divide people into categories of worthy and unworthy, friend and enemy, insider and outsider. Love quietly dissolves those boundaries. It invites us to see one another through gentler eyes. The farther we travel into love, the more difficult it becomes to exclude anyone from our compassion.

The deepest measure of a spiritual life is not how much we know, how certain we are, or how impressive we appear. It is the degree to which love has taken root within us. Wherever love grows, divine life is already present. Wherever love is shared, the Light continues to shine.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Fill me with love*
>
> Exhale: *Let it flow outward*
