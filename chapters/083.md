## 83 — The Open Side

> *"Immediately blood and water came out."* — John 19:34

The heart of divine love is not closed.

Human beings often protect themselves by building walls. We hide our wounds. We guard our vulnerabilities. We learn to keep certain parts of ourselves beyond reach, hoping that distance will spare us from pain.

Yet love moves in the opposite direction. Love opens itself.

The image of an opened side speaks of a love that withholds nothing. It is a love willing to be seen, willing to be touched, willing to pour itself out for the life of the world. Such love is not weakness. It is courage in its purest form.

Blood and water evoke the mystery of life continually being given. The universe itself is sustained by generosity. Every breath, every sunrise, every act of compassion arrives as gift. Beneath existence is a current of self-giving love that never ceases to flow.

As we awaken to this reality, we may find our own hearts slowly opening. Not all at once, but gradually. We become less defended, less fearful, more capable of tenderness. We learn that healing often begins where openness replaces self-protection.

The invitation is not to become wounded, but to become available—to allow love to move through us as freely as it moves toward us. The heart that opens discovers that divine life has been flowing there all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my heart*
>
> Exhale: *Let love flow through me*
