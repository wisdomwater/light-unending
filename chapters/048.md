## 48 — Blindness and Certainty

> *"Those who don't see may see; and those who see may become blind."* — John 9:39

There is a kind of blindness that has nothing to do with the eyes.

It appears whenever we become so certain that we stop listening, so convinced that we stop learning, so confident in our conclusions that we lose our sense of wonder. The mind naturally seeks certainty because certainty feels safe. It gives us solid ground beneath our feet. Yet the deepest realities of life, love, and God are often larger than our explanations.

The spiritual journey requires a different kind of seeing. It begins with the humility to admit that there is always more to discover. The moment we believe we have fully grasped the mystery, the mystery slips beyond our reach. The Infinite cannot be contained within our concepts. Love is always deeper than our definitions.

This does not mean that truth is unknowable. Rather, it means that truth is living. We encounter it not merely through ideas but through relationship, experience, and transformation. The heart grows wiser not by collecting certainty, but by remaining open. Like a window turned toward the morning sun, it receives light because it is willing to receive.

There is a hidden freedom in saying, "I do not fully know." Such honesty creates space for growth. It softens our judgments toward others and toward ourselves. It allows us to meet life with curiosity instead of fear. The soul no longer needs to defend every position because it rests in something deeper than being right.

Perhaps the clearest vision belongs not to those who claim to see everything, but to those who remain teachable. They move through the world with open hands and open hearts, ready to be surprised by grace. And in that openness, new light continues to enter, revealing ever wider horizons of love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Make me teachable*
>
> Exhale: *Open me to mystery*
