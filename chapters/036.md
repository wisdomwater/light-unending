## 36 — The Bread That Satisfies

> *"Whoever comes to me will not be hungry."* — John 6:35

There is a hunger that food cannot satisfy. We feel it in our longing for meaning, belonging, peace, and love. We pursue achievement, approval, possessions, experiences, and certainty, hoping that one more accomplishment or one more answer will finally quiet the restless ache within us.

Yet the soul was made for something deeper.

Many of us spend years searching outside ourselves for what can only be received from a deeper source. We look for fulfillment in things that were never meant to carry the weight of our deepest desires. Even good things can become burdens when we ask them to provide what only love can give.

The invitation of the spiritual life is not merely to believe certain ideas. It is to learn how to live from a different center. Beneath the noise of striving and comparison, there is a living Presence continually offering itself to us. It nourishes what is most real within us. It reminds us that our worth is not earned, our belonging is not conditional, and our life is held within something larger than our fears.

When we begin to draw our life from this deeper source, something changes. The hunger does not disappear because we have acquired everything we want. It softens because we discover that we are already connected to what we truly need. We stop grasping so tightly. We stop chasing fulfillment as though it were always somewhere else.

The bread of life is not a reward waiting at the end of the journey. It is a gift continually offered in the present moment. Every breath, every act of love, every glimpse of beauty, every moment of genuine connection becomes a reminder that divine life is already flowing toward us. The invitation is simply to receive it.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Feed my soul*
>
> Exhale: *With living love*
