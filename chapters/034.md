## 34 — Gathering the Fragments

> *"Gather up the broken pieces... that nothing be lost."* — John 6:12

Many of us carry fragments of our lives that seem disconnected from the whole. There are forgotten dreams, old wounds, unfinished chapters, relationships that ended badly, and seasons we would rather not revisit. We often assume that spiritual growth means leaving these pieces behind and moving on.

Yet love does not discard so easily.

The deeper movement of healing is often not the rejection of what came before, but its gentle gathering. What we once considered useless may still contain wisdom. What we regarded as failure may become compassion. What felt like wasted years may become the very soil from which new life emerges.

The Divine Presence works with the entirety of our lives. Nothing is hidden from its gaze, and nothing is beyond its reach. The joyful moments belong. The painful moments belong. The parts of ourselves we celebrate and the parts we struggle to accept are all held within a love larger than our understanding.

This does not mean every experience was good. Some things wounded us deeply. Some losses remain difficult to name. Yet even in places where brokenness has touched us, there remains the possibility of redemption. The fragments are not gathered because they were perfect. They are gathered because they are precious.

Perhaps this is one of the great mysteries of grace: nothing true is ever ultimately wasted. Love continues its patient work, collecting the scattered pieces of our lives and weaving them into a greater wholeness. What appears broken from one perspective may, in time, reveal itself as part of a larger beauty we could not yet see.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Gather my fragments*
>
> Exhale: *Make me whole*
