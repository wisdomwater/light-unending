## 90 — Follow Me

> *"What is that to you? You follow me."* — John 21:22

One of the great temptations of life is comparison. We look at the journeys of others and wonder why their path seems easier, clearer, or more meaningful than our own. We compare gifts, opportunities, successes, struggles, and even spiritual experiences. Before long, our attention drifts away from the life we have been given and becomes entangled in lives that are not ours to live.

Yet the voice of Love continually calls us back.

The spiritual path is deeply personal. No two souls travel it in exactly the same way. Some walk through seasons of abundance, while others learn wisdom through loss. Some find God in silence, while others find God in service. Some move quickly, while others take slow and winding roads. The invitation is not to understand another person's journey, but to be faithful to our own.

When we become preoccupied with comparison, we often miss the Presence that is already before us. We become spectators of other lives instead of participants in our own. The mind asks endless questions about what should be happening. Love gently returns us to what is happening now.

There is great freedom in releasing the need to measure our progress against anyone else's. The Divine does not mass-produce human beings. Each life carries a unique expression of beauty, purpose, and possibility. What is being formed within you cannot be formed in quite the same way within another. The path beneath your feet is holy because it is yours.

The invitation to follow is not a demand but a gentle summons. It is the call to trust that Love knows the way forward, even when the destination remains hidden. One step at a time, one day at a time, we learn to place our attention less on the journeys around us and more on the Presence walking beside us.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Guide my path*
>
> Exhale: *I will follow*
