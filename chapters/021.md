## 21 — Meeting God at the Well

> *"Whoever drinks of the water that I will give him will never thirst again."* — John 4:14

There are seasons in life when we find ourselves returning again and again to the same wells. We seek fulfillment through achievement, approval, comfort, certainty, relationships, possessions, or endless distraction. For a moment they satisfy us, yet before long the thirst returns. Something within us quietly longs for a deeper source of life.

The sacred often meets us precisely in these ordinary places. Not in moments of spiritual triumph, but in the middle of daily routines, familiar disappointments, and unmet longings. We arrive carrying our burdens, our questions, our history, and our weariness. Yet beneath the surface of our searching, a deeper Presence has already been waiting for us.

Many of us fear that if we were fully seen, we would be rejected. We hide parts of ourselves behind accomplishments, identities, explanations, and carefully maintained appearances. Yet divine love does not turn away from what is true. It meets us with a gaze that sees everything and still remains. What feels exposed in the light becomes healed in the light.

The living water we seek is not something we earn or manufacture. It is the life of God already flowing beneath the surface of our existence. We do not create the spring. We discover it. Beneath the noise of striving, beneath the ache of loneliness, beneath the endless effort to become enough, there is a well that has never run dry.

As we learn to drink from this deeper source, something begins to change. The frantic search for completion softens. Fear loosens its grip. We become less concerned with proving ourselves and more available to love. The water that refreshes us begins to overflow toward others. What we receive becomes something we naturally share.

The invitation remains simple: come as you are. Bring your thirst. Bring your questions. Bring the whole story of your life. The well is deeper than your failures, deeper than your wounds, deeper even than your understanding. And there, waiting beneath everything, is the living Presence that has been seeking you all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Living water within*
>
> Exhale: *Quench my deepest thirst*
