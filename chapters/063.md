## 63 — Rooms in the Father's House

> *"In my Father’s house are many homes."* — John 14:2

There are moments in life when we wonder if there is truly a place for us. We carry wounds, regrets, doubts, and questions. We compare ourselves to others and quietly fear that we do not belong. The heart can become convinced that love is reserved for those who are more certain, more successful, more spiritual, or more worthy than we are.

Yet the invitation of divine love moves in the opposite direction. It does not narrow the doorway. It widens the welcome. The image of a house filled with many rooms speaks of abundance rather than scarcity. There is space enough for every story, every personality, every stage of the journey. No one is asked to become someone else before entering.

Perhaps the deepest longing of the human soul is not merely to arrive somewhere after death, but to discover that we are already held within a greater belonging. The house of God is not a distant location hidden beyond the stars. It is the vast reality of divine love in which we already live and move, though we often forget it. We awaken to it gradually, like someone opening their eyes in a home they did not realize they had never left.

The spiritual journey is often less about earning a place and more about trusting that a place has already been prepared. Love moves toward us before we know how to move toward love. Grace precedes every step. The door stands open long before we think to knock.

When we begin to see others through this lens, something changes within us. We stop dividing the world into insiders and outsiders. We become less interested in guarding the rooms and more interested in helping people find their way home. The heart grows larger. Compassion becomes easier. We begin to recognize that the same love that welcomes us is welcoming everyone.

There is room for you. Room for your questions. Room for your healing. Room for your becoming. The house of divine love is wider than fear imagines, and the invitation has always been yours.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *There is room for me*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Your love*
