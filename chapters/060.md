## 60 — Washed by Grace

> *"If I don’t wash you, you have no part with me."* — John 13:8

Many of us find it easier to give love than to receive it. We are willing to help others, support others, and carry burdens for others. Yet when it comes time to admit our own need, something within us resists. We want to remain strong, self-sufficient, and in control.

The spiritual life often begins to deepen when we discover that we cannot heal ourselves through effort alone. There are wounds we cannot mend, fears we cannot untangle, and burdens we cannot carry forever. At some point, we must allow ourselves to be helped. We must allow ourselves to be loved.

Grace arrives before we are ready for it. It comes before we have everything figured out. It reaches us while we are still uncertain, still struggling, still becoming. Divine love does not wait for perfection. It meets us exactly where we are and gently invites us forward.

This can feel vulnerable. To receive love is to lower our defenses. It is to stop pretending that we have everything under control. Yet beneath that vulnerability lies freedom. The heart no longer has to earn its belonging. It can simply rest in the reality that it is already held.

Perhaps this is why grace feels so surprising. We expect a transaction, but encounter a gift. We expect a demand, but encounter an embrace. Again and again, the sacred invitation is not merely to love others, but to allow ourselves to be loved as well.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I receive your grace*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in your love*
