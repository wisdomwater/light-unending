## 84 — The Dawn Beyond Despair

> *"Now on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene went early, while it was still dark..."* — John 20:1

There are moments in life when we rise before the dawn carrying grief we cannot fix. We move through the darkness on habit alone, showing up because love compels us, even when hope seems lost. We expect only silence. We expect only the familiar weight of sorrow. Yet many of the deepest movements of grace begin while it is still dark.

We often imagine transformation arriving with certainty, clarity, and confidence. More often it arrives quietly, hidden beneath confusion and tears. The heart cannot always recognize what is being born within it. What feels like an ending may be the threshold of a beginning. What appears to be absence may conceal a Presence nearer than we know.

The empty tomb speaks to a mystery woven throughout the spiritual life: love cannot be contained by what has died. We experience losses, disappointments, shattered expectations, and seasons when old certainties fall away. Yet the Life that sustains all things continues moving beneath the surface. Divine love is not defeated by darkness, failure, or even death itself. It keeps creating, renewing, and awakening.

Many of us spend great energy trying to return to what once was. We long for a previous chapter, a former version of ourselves, a life untouched by pain. But the invitation is rarely backward. The sacred often meets us by opening a future we did not expect. The stone is already rolled away before we arrive. Grace is already at work before we know to look for it.

Every dawn carries this promise. No night lasts forever. No sorrow has the final word. Beneath the surface of every ending, life is quietly preparing to emerge again. The Light continues shining, even when our eyes have not yet adjusted enough to see it.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Even in darkness*
>
> Exhale: *Your light is near*
