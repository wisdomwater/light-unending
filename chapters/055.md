## 55 — Resurrection Begins Now

> *"I am the resurrection and the life."* — John 11:25

Many people imagine resurrection as something that belongs entirely to the future. It is seen as a distant promise, waiting somewhere beyond death and beyond time. Yet the invitation of the spiritual life is not merely to hope for life someday. It is to awaken to Life here and now.

There are ways of living that slowly diminish the soul. Fear narrows our vision. Resentment hardens the heart. Despair convinces us that nothing can change. We may continue moving through our days, yet inwardly feel disconnected from the vitality and wonder that once animated us. The loss is not always physical. Sometimes it is the loss of aliveness itself.

The Life of God continually moves toward restoration. Like sunlight reaching through a window, it quietly seeks every hidden corner. Love does not force its way into our lives, but it never stops inviting us. Again and again, grace calls us beyond old patterns, beyond old wounds, beyond the stories that tell us we are trapped.

Resurrection begins whenever we say yes to that invitation. It begins when forgiveness softens what bitterness has hardened. It begins when compassion overcomes indifference. It begins when we risk hope after disappointment. Each movement toward greater love is a participation in the Life that is always renewing the world.

This is why spiritual awakening often feels less like acquiring something new and more like remembering something ancient. Beneath our fears and defenses, Life has already been present. The divine image within us has never disappeared. It has only waited to be uncovered and expressed more fully.

The promise of resurrection is not merely that life triumphs at the end of the story. It is that Life is present within the story even now. In every act of love, every moment of healing, every step toward wholeness, eternity quietly touches the present moment.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Life is here*
>
> Exhale: *I awaken within it*
