## 76 — Glory Shared

> *"The glory which you have given me, I have given to them."* — John 17:22

Many of us were taught to think of glory as something distant and unreachable—a quality belonging to saints, heroes, or heaven itself. We imagine it as a brightness reserved for a select few while the rest of us live ordinary lives far below it.

Yet divine glory is not a reward handed out for spiritual achievement. It is the radiance of a life fully rooted in love. It is what shines through a person when fear loosens its grip and the heart becomes transparent to grace.

Most of us have glimpsed this glory without calling it by that name. We see it in a grandmother's kindness, in a friend's faithful presence during grief, in the quiet courage of someone who chooses compassion when anger would be easier. Such moments carry a light that feels larger than the individual expressing it.

The tragedy is not that we lack this light, but that we so often forget it. We become preoccupied with our failures, our comparisons, and our unfinished journeys. We search for worthiness while the deeper truth waits patiently beneath our striving. The divine image has never left us. The sacred flame still burns beneath the ashes.

The spiritual path is less about acquiring glory than uncovering it. As we open ourselves to love, the hidden light becomes visible. We begin to recognize that what we admire in others is not foreign to us. The same Source that flows through them flows through us as well.

The invitation is not to become someone else. It is to become fully yourself—so deeply rooted in love that the light within can finally shine without obstruction. What has been given does not need to be earned. It only needs to be received.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your light lives within me*
>
> Exhale: *Let it shine through love*
