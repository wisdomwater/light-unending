## 50 — The Shepherd Who Knows Your Name

> *“I know my own, and I’m known by my own.”* — John 10:14

There is something deeply healing about being truly known. Most of us spend parts of our lives hiding behind roles, accomplishments, failures, expectations, or carefully constructed identities. We fear that if others saw everything within us, they might turn away. Yet beneath this fear lives a longing to be fully seen and fully loved at the same time.

Divine love meets us precisely there.

The image of the shepherd speaks of a relationship far more intimate than distant observation. We are not anonymous faces in a crowd or forgotten names on a list. We are known from the inside out. Every joy, every sorrow, every hidden wound, every unspoken hope rests within a Presence that understands more deeply than we understand ourselves.

This knowing is not the gaze of judgment but of compassion. Love sees our confusion and does not abandon us. Love sees our contradictions and remains patient. Love sees both our brokenness and our beauty, holding them together within a larger vision of who we are becoming. Nothing needs to be hidden because nothing can separate us from that care.

As we grow quieter within, we begin to recognize this voice for ourselves. It does not usually arrive through fear, coercion, or shame. More often it appears as a gentle pull toward what is true, life-giving, and loving. It leads rather than pushes. It invites rather than demands. It calls us toward greater freedom, greater wholeness, and greater participation in the life of love.

To hear this voice is not merely to receive guidance. It is to remember who we are. Beneath all the labels we have collected through life, there remains a deeper identity that has never been lost. We belong to the One who knows us completely and loves us still. The journey of faith may be, in part, the gradual awakening to that simple and beautiful truth.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You know me completely*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Your love*
