## 89 — Do You Love Me?

> *"Lord, you know everything. You know that I have affection for you."* — John 21:17

There are moments when life strips away our carefully constructed image of ourselves. We discover that we are not as strong, faithful, patient, or courageous as we once believed. We fail in ways we never expected. We speak words we regret. We retreat when we hoped we would stand firm. In those moments, shame often rushes in to tell us that our story is over.

Yet Love asks a different question.

The deepest spiritual question is not whether we have performed perfectly. It is not whether we have understood everything correctly or lived without mistakes. Beneath all the noise, beneath all the striving, there remains a simple invitation: Do you love? Even if your love feels small, imperfect, or fragile, are you willing to turn your heart once again toward the One who has never turned away from you?

Many of us imagine that God's gaze is fixed upon our failures. We carry old disappointments as though they define us. But Divine Love sees more deeply than our worst moments. It sees the longing beneath the fear. It sees the goodness beneath the confusion. It sees the true self that remains present even when we lose our way.

The healing of the soul often begins when we stop defending ourselves and simply allow ourselves to be known. We stand honestly before Love with empty hands and unfinished lives. There is no need for elaborate explanations. The One who knows everything already knows the sincerity hidden within our trembling hearts.

And from that place of acceptance comes a new beginning. Not because the past is ignored, but because love is stronger than the past. Grace does not merely forgive; it restores. Again and again, Divine Love invites us beyond shame and back into relationship, reminding us that every ending can become the doorway to a deeper life.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You know my heart*
>
> Exhale: *And still you love me*
