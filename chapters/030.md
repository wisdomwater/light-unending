## 30 — Do You Want to Be Whole

> *"Do you want to be made well?"* — John 5:6

There is a difference between wanting relief and wanting wholeness.

Relief asks for the pain to stop. Wholeness invites us into a deeper transformation. Most of us readily recognize our suffering, but we may not always recognize the ways we have adapted ourselves around it. We build habits, defenses, identities, and stories that help us survive. Over time, what once protected us can become a place where we remain stuck.

The question arises gently, not as a demand but as an invitation. Do you want to be whole? Not perfect. Not flawless. Not finished. Whole. Do you want to live from a deeper center than fear? Do you want to release the burdens you have carried for so long that they now feel like part of who you are?

Wholeness begins when we become willing to tell the truth. We acknowledge our wounds without becoming defined by them. We stop pretending that what diminishes life is somehow giving us life. We stop waiting for someone else to rescue us from the work that only we can consent to. The moment of transformation often begins with simple honesty.

Yet even this honesty is held within grace. We are not asked to heal ourselves through effort alone. The healing presence of God is already moving within us, drawing us toward life. Love patiently calls us beyond resignation, beyond self-protection, beyond the belief that things can never change. What seems impossible from the perspective of fear becomes possible in the presence of love.

The path to wholeness is rarely completed in a single moment. It unfolds step by step, day by day, choice by choice. Yet every movement toward greater openness, compassion, and trust participates in that healing work. Each small yes creates space for more life to emerge.

The invitation remains before us today. Not as pressure. Not as judgment. Simply as a doorway. Beneath all the noise and striving, Love asks again: Do you want to be whole? And whenever we answer yes, however quietly, the journey has already begun.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Make me whole*
>
> Exhale: *I open to love*
