## 53 — The Love That Waits

> *"Now Jesus loved Martha, and her sister, and Lazarus."* — John 11:5

Few experiences are more difficult than waiting for help that does not seem to come. We pray, hope, trust, and watch the horizon. Yet sometimes the answer we long for remains absent. The healing does not arrive. The situation does not change. The door remains closed. In those moments, it is easy to assume that delay means indifference.

Yet love and immediacy are not always the same thing.

There are seasons when life appears silent, but beneath that silence something deeper is unfolding. The seed spends time hidden beneath the soil before it breaks the surface. The dawn begins long before the first light becomes visible. What appears absent on the surface may still be quietly present in ways we cannot yet see.

Much of our suffering comes from believing that love must always act according to our timetable. We want certainty, clarity, and resolution. We want the story to move forward. Yet the deeper movements of life often unfold at a pace beyond our control. The soul matures slowly. Healing has its seasons. Wisdom rarely arrives all at once.

This does not mean that pain is good or that grief should be welcomed. Even the deepest spiritual life does not require us to pretend that loss is easy. Love meets us honestly in our sorrow. It sits beside us in our confusion. It hears every question and receives every tear. Divine Presence is not waiting for us on the other side of our struggle. It is already here within it.

Perhaps the invitation is not to understand the waiting, but to trust that we are held within it. The Love that accompanies us has not forgotten us. Even when we cannot see the next step, we are not abandoned. Even when the path disappears into mystery, we remain surrounded by a Presence more faithful than our fears.

Sometimes what feels like absence is simply love working at a depth we cannot yet perceive.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I am held in love*
>
> Exhale: *Even in the waiting*
