## 7 — Grace Upon Grace

> *The divine life comes to us not in measured portions, but in an endless flow of gift.*

Many of us carry the feeling that we must earn our place in life. We work for approval, strive for success, and quietly wonder whether we have done enough. Even in our spiritual lives, we can fall into the habit of believing that love must be deserved and acceptance must be secured through effort. The heart becomes weary from trying to prove its worth.

Yet there is another way of seeing. Beneath the logic of earning and deserving lies the deeper reality of grace. Grace is the gift that arrives before we are ready. It is the kindness that meets us before we have learned to ask for it. It is the quiet generosity woven into existence itself—the sunlight that falls on every field, the breath that fills our lungs, the love that continues reaching toward us even when we are turned away.

Often we imagine grace as a rare event, something given only in extraordinary moments. But grace is far more abundant than that. It meets us in countless ordinary ways: through a word of encouragement, an unexpected opportunity, a moment of forgiveness, a beautiful morning, a second chance, or the simple realization that we are still here and still being invited into life. What we call ordinary is often saturated with gifts we have learned to overlook.

There are times when life wounds us deeply, and we may wonder whether grace has abandoned us. Yet even then, another grace appears. Strength arrives for a day we did not think we could endure. Comfort comes through the presence of another person. Wisdom grows from experiences we would never have chosen. Slowly we discover that grace is not the absence of difficulty but the presence of Love within it.

The spiritual journey is, in many ways, a growing awareness of this continual giving. We begin to notice that every step has been supported by gifts we did not create. Every breath, every insight, every act of healing, every movement toward wholeness has been carried by a generosity larger than ourselves. We are sustained by blessings layered upon blessings, mercy upon mercy, grace upon grace.

When we awaken to this abundance, gratitude naturally begins to bloom. We no longer live as though we must force life to yield its treasures. Instead, we learn to receive. The heart softens. Trust deepens. And we discover that the Love at the center of all things has never stopped pouring itself out into the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Gift upon gift*
>
> Exhale: *Grace upon grace*
