## 14 — Water Becoming Wine

> *"You have kept the good wine until now!"* — John 2:10

Much of life is spent noticing what has run out. We become aware of our limitations, our disappointments, our exhaustion, our unanswered questions. There are seasons when the joy that once seemed abundant feels distant, and we quietly wonder whether there is enough left to sustain us.

Yet divine grace often meets us precisely at the point of perceived lack.

The water jars stand as a reminder of ordinary things—common vessels filled with common water. Nothing about them appears remarkable. Yet the sacred often works this way. Transformation rarely begins with something extraordinary. It begins with what is already present. The ordinary moments of our lives, the simple offerings we bring, the small acts of faithfulness we barely notice may become the very places where grace reveals itself.

Many of us imagine spiritual growth as acquiring something we do not possess. We search for greater wisdom, deeper experiences, stronger faith, or clearer certainty. Yet the movement of the soul is often less about acquisition and more about awakening. The water was already there. The vessels were already there. What changed was not the presence of grace, but the unveiling of what grace could become.

There is also a beautiful surprise hidden within this story: the best wine appears later, not earlier. We often fear that our finest days lie behind us. We assume youth, certainty, or earlier chapters contained life's richest treasures. But love does not operate according to scarcity. The Divine is endlessly creative, continually drawing new life from places we thought were empty. There are depths of joy, compassion, wisdom, and union that can emerge long after we believe the celebration is winding down.

The invitation is simple: bring what you have. Bring the ordinary water of your daily life. Bring your questions, your weariness, your hopes, and your unfinished becoming. Trust that the One who dwells quietly within all things is still transforming the commonplace into something beautiful. The feast is not ending. Grace may be preparing its finest gift even now.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Transform what I bring*
>
> Exhale: *Into living joy*
