## 35 — Walking Through the Storm

> *"It is I. Don't be afraid."* — John 6:20

There are seasons when life feels steady and familiar, and there are seasons when the winds rise without warning. Plans unravel. Certainties dissolve. We find ourselves rowing against waves we never expected to face. In such moments, fear often arrives before wisdom does.

Storms have a way of narrowing our vision. We become consumed by the immediate threat, the unanswered question, the uncertain future. The mind searches desperately for solid ground while the heart grows weary from the effort of holding everything together.

Yet there is a Presence that does not disappear when the waters become rough. It is often in the darkness, when our own strength begins to fail, that we become aware of a deeper companionship. Not a distant rescuer standing safely on the shore, but a Presence drawing near in the midst of the waves themselves.

The voice of love rarely shouts above the storm. Instead, it speaks quietly beneath it. Again and again, it whispers the same invitation: Do not be afraid. Not because the storm is unreal. Not because pain does not matter. But because fear is not the deepest truth about your life. Beneath every wave, beneath every uncertainty, you are held by something stronger than what threatens you.

The goal of the spiritual journey is not to avoid every storm. It is to discover the One who remains present within them. Over time, we learn that peace is not found in controlling the sea. Peace is found in trusting the Presence that never leaves us, whether the waters are calm or wild.

Perhaps today you do not need all the answers. Perhaps it is enough to hear the quiet assurance that has echoed through countless storms before yours: You are not alone. Love is nearer than you think.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are here*
>
> Exhale: *I will not fear*
