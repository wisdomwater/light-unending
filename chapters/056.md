## 56 — Unbinding One Another

> *"Free him, and let him go."* — John 11:44

Even after stepping out of the tomb, Lazarus is still wrapped in grave clothes.

This is often how transformation unfolds in our own lives. We may experience a profound awakening, a moment of healing, a new clarity, or a fresh beginning. Yet the habits, fears, wounds, and old identities that once bound us do not always fall away immediately. New life may be present, even while remnants of the old life still cling to us.

Many of us carry invisible wrappings. Some are woven from shame. Some are made of fear, self-judgment, or the belief that we are unworthy of love. Others come from painful experiences that taught us to hide parts of ourselves from the world. These bindings can feel so familiar that we mistake them for who we are.

One of the great gifts of community is that we help unbind one another. We remind each other of truths that are difficult to remember alone. We offer encouragement when someone has forgotten their strength. We create spaces where healing can continue unfolding. Rarely do we walk fully into freedom by ourselves.

This kind of unbinding does not happen through condemnation or pressure. Love loosens knots that force only tightens. Compassion creates room for growth. Patience allows healing to mature at its own pace. We become instruments of grace whenever we help another person move from fear toward trust, from isolation toward belonging, from shame toward dignity.

The deeper spiritual journey is not simply about coming alive ourselves. It is also about becoming people who help others come alive. As we are freed, we learn to participate in the freeing of others. The love we receive naturally becomes the love we offer.

Life is continually calling people out of their tombs. One of the holiest things we can do is help remove the wrappings and make space for them to walk freely into the light.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Free me in love*
>
> Exhale: *Help me free others*
