## 15 — The Joy Hidden in Ordinary Things

> *"Fill the water pots with water."* — John 2:7

We often imagine that the sacred will arrive through dramatic moments—great revelations, extraordinary experiences, or unmistakable signs. Yet much of life unfolds in quieter ways. Most days are made of ordinary tasks, familiar routines, simple conversations, and small acts of care. We pass through them so quickly that we rarely notice the quiet holiness they contain.

There is a temptation to believe that joy lives somewhere else. We tell ourselves that fulfillment will come when circumstances improve, when our questions are answered, or when life finally becomes what we hoped it would be. In the meantime, we overlook the gifts already present. We miss the warmth of a shared meal, the beauty of morning light, the comfort of a friend's voice, or the simple miracle of another breath.

The spiritual life is often an awakening to what has been here all along. The Divine is not waiting only at the end of the journey. Presence meets us in kitchens and offices, in gardens and grocery stores, in laughter and tears. Love quietly fills the ordinary moments we tend to dismiss. The sacred is woven into the fabric of everyday life.

When we begin to pay attention, the world changes. Not because reality becomes different, but because our eyes become more open. What once seemed commonplace begins to shimmer with meaning. We discover that joy is less something we chase and more something we learn to notice. Beneath the surface of ordinary existence, grace is continually flowing.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to slow down enough to see. The cup of coffee in your hands, the person standing before you, the work entrusted to you, the air entering your lungs—none of these are merely ordinary. Every moment carries the possibility of encounter. Every moment can become a doorway. The joy we seek may already be quietly waiting within the life we are living.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *To hidden joy*
