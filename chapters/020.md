## 20 — Stepping Into the Light

> *"He who does the truth comes to the light."* — John 3:21

There is a part of us that longs to be fully seen. Beneath the masks we wear, beneath the stories we tell, beneath the careful image we present to the world, there is a quiet desire to live honestly and freely. Yet there is often another part that hesitates. We fear what might be revealed if we step into the light.

The light of God is not a harsh spotlight searching for flaws. It is more like the gentle dawn that slowly fills a darkened room. It reveals what is there, not to shame us, but to help us see clearly. What has been hidden can finally breathe. What has been wounded can finally heal.

Many of us spend tremendous energy protecting ourselves from exposure. We hide our mistakes, conceal our fears, and avoid the places within that feel unfinished or broken. Yet what remains hidden often retains its power over us. Healing begins when we find the courage to bring our whole selves into the presence of love.

This movement toward the light is not about becoming perfect. It is about becoming real. The spiritual life invites us to stop pretending and to rest in the truth of who we are. We discover that we do not need to earn acceptance before we can approach God. The light itself becomes the place where transformation happens.

Again and again, life offers us opportunities to step forward. To speak honestly. To forgive. To ask for help. To release an old burden. Each small act of openness becomes a doorway through which more light can enter. What once felt frightening gradually becomes liberating.

The light has never been our enemy. It has been patiently waiting for us all along. And when we finally step into it, we often discover that what we feared would lead to rejection instead becomes the beginning of freedom.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Lead me into the light*
>
> Exhale: *I have nothing to hide*
