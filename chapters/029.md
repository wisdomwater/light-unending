## 29 — Waiting Beside the Pool

> *"Do you want to be made well?"* — John 5:6

There are seasons of life when we find ourselves waiting beside a pool that never seems to stir.

We wait for circumstances to change, for healing to arrive, for relationships to mend, for clarity to appear. We tell ourselves that life will begin once the right opportunity comes, once the right person helps us, once the conditions finally become favorable. Years can pass this way. The heart quietly settles into resignation, expecting little and hoping carefully.

Yet beneath our waiting, a deeper question is often being asked.

Do we truly desire wholeness? Not merely relief from discomfort, but a transformation that invites us into a new way of living. Sometimes we become so accustomed to our wounds, our fears, or our familiar patterns that healing itself can feel unsettling. To become well may require releasing old identities, old stories, and old reasons for remaining where we are.

The divine invitation does not always arrive through the channels we expect. We imagine that help will come from a particular source, through a specific process, or according to a plan we have carefully constructed. Yet grace often appears from an unexpected direction. While we are watching the water, Love may already be standing beside us.

The good news is that we do not have to earn our way to the front of the line. We do not have to compete for God's attention or wait for a rare moment of favor. Divine Presence is not limited, scarce, or reserved for a fortunate few. It is already here, meeting us in the very place where we have grown weary from waiting.

The journey toward wholeness begins when we dare to respond. A small step. A quiet trust. A willingness to rise when Love calls our name. What seemed impossible for years may begin with a single moment of openness. The healing itself is a gift, but so is the discovery that we were never abandoned beside the pool. The One we were waiting for had been seeking us all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Love meets me here*
>
> Exhale: *I rise and trust*
