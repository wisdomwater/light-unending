## 69 — Remaining

> *"Remain in me, and I in you."* — John 15:4

Many of us imagine the spiritual life as a long journey toward a distant destination. We think we must climb higher, learn more, or become more worthy before we can truly encounter the Divine. Yet the invitation is surprisingly simple: remain.

Remaining is not striving. It is not forcing ourselves into a spiritual experience. It is the gentle practice of returning again and again to what is already here. Like sitting quietly beside a loved one, it is less about accomplishing something and more about consenting to a relationship that already exists.

We drift so easily. Our attention is pulled into worries about tomorrow, regrets about yesterday, and endless judgments about ourselves. We become lost in thoughts about life rather than living it. Yet each moment offers a doorway back. A breath. A pause. A glimpse of beauty. A quiet awareness that we are not alone.

To remain is to trust that the Presence we seek is already seeking us. Long before we turn toward God, God is turned toward us. Long before we remember the sacred, the sacred remembers us. The relationship does not begin with our effort. It begins with grace.

Little by little, this practice of returning changes us. Not through pressure or perfection, but through familiarity. We begin to recognize the quiet companionship that has always surrounded us. We discover that what we thought was distance was often distraction, and what we feared was absence was simply our forgetting.

The invitation today is wonderfully uncomplicated. Return. Rest. Remain. The Love that sustains all things is nearer than you imagine, waiting not for achievement but for attention.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I return to You*
>
> Exhale: *You remain with me*
