## 31 — Rise and Walk

> *"Arise, take up your mat, and walk."* — John 5:8

There are moments when life narrows around our limitations. We become aware of what has been lost, what remains unresolved, or what seems impossible to change. The longer we live with a burden, the more natural it can feel to remain where we are. What began as a temporary resting place slowly becomes home.

Yet the voice of Love does not speak to us as our fears speak. Fear points to weakness, scarcity, and impossibility. Love speaks to the deeper truth hidden beneath the surface. It sees possibilities we cannot yet see. It calls forth life before we fully believe life is possible.

The invitation to rise is not a demand to strive harder or prove our worth. It is an awakening. Something within us recognizes that we are more than our wounds, more than our failures, and more than the stories we have carried about ourselves. The deepest part of our being has always remained connected to the source of life, even when we have forgotten.

Taking up the mat is part of the journey. The places where we struggled do not disappear from our history. Our scars remain part of our story. Yet what once held us down no longer defines us. What was once a symbol of limitation becomes a reminder of grace. We carry the memory, but we are no longer carried by it.

Then comes the simple act of walking. One step. Then another. Most transformation unfolds this way. Rarely through dramatic certainty, but through small acts of trust repeated over time. We discover that the strength we needed arrives as we begin to move.

Love is always calling us toward life. Not someday, when everything is perfect. Not after every question is answered. Now. In this moment. The invitation is already being spoken within the depths of the heart: Rise and walk. The path appears as we begin.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Call me into life*
>
> Exhale: *I rise and walk*
