## 45 — Before Abraham Was

> *"Before Abraham came into existence, I AM."* — John 8:58

Much of our lives is spent moving between memories of the past and concerns about the future. We revisit old wounds, replay conversations, imagine different outcomes, and worry about what may come next. The mind is constantly traveling through time, searching for certainty in places where certainty can never quite be found.

Yet beneath all the movement of thought there is a deeper reality that simply is.

The divine presence is not confined to yesterday or tomorrow. It is not waiting at the end of our spiritual journey, nor locked away in some distant beginning. It is here. Closer than breath. Nearer than our own thoughts. The sacred mystery meets us not primarily in what has been or what might be, but in the living fullness of this moment.

This is why moments of genuine stillness can feel so profound. For a brief time, the noise settles. The endless narration quiets. We stop trying to become someone else or arrive somewhere else. We simply awaken to what has always been present. What we discover is not emptiness but presence. Not absence but fullness. Not distance but intimacy.

The great spiritual invitation is not to escape the world but to become present within it. Every act of attention becomes a doorway. Every breath becomes a reminder. Every ordinary moment carries within it the possibility of encountering the Eternal shining through the temporary.

The One who is beyond all ages and generations is also present in this very moment. The light that illuminated the saints, the seekers, and the wanderers of every age shines now as well. It shines within every human heart, patiently calling us to awaken, to trust, and to participate more deeply in the life of love.

The mystery of God is older than every story we tell and nearer than every word we speak. Beneath all that changes, there remains the quiet and enduring reality of the Eternal Presence saying, simply, *I Am.*

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Here in this moment*
>
> Exhale: *You are present*
